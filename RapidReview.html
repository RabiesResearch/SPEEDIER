The table below provides a review of documents and literature, with strengths and limitations highlighted alongside the relevant responses and feedback for SPEEDIER/FETPAFI staff. Links to individual documents are available for orange titled entries.
A full version of the table can be downloaded here.


Title Strengths Limitations Responses/Feedback (FETPAFI/SPEEDIER Technical Staff)
WHO Expert Consultation on Rabies: WHO TRS 1012 Aimed to focus on a country-centric approach to support, empower and catalyse national entities to control and eliminate rabies.

Did not mention monitoring and evaluation mechanisms per country to report the status of each member state.

Given the different context per country, there still be differences on implementing the post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) in countries where Gavi runs other vaccine programmes.

So for countries not being run by Gavi on other vaccine programmes, will they not be included in this intervention? 		WHO is conducting a regular meeting and consultations with other international experts to dicuss about dog mediated rabies.
National Rabies Prevention and Control Program Manual of Operations (2012) This manual of operations is comprehensive in terms of addressing the pressing issue on rabies on both human and and animal rabies. This manual of operation was made on 2012 and perhaps needs to be updated to cover new data, findings, provisions, or international guidelines that it can incorporate in the updated version.

According to the recent news, there is a updated version of the manual dated last year, 2018. But this document has still yet to be disseminated because the Department of Health (DOH) and Department of Agriculture (DA) is still coordinating on some matters.

The Rabies program in Fourmula +1 of the DOH is still the running program being followed by DOH.

The names of various committees and other government bodies has also been revised in the updated operations of manual.
Republic Act No. 9482: An Act Providing for the Control and Elimination of Human and Animal Rabies National law that is collaborative across several government agencies and non-government organizations : Department of Health (DOH), Department of Agriculture (DA), Department of Education (DepEd), Local Government Units, and academe. Despite the law, at the end of the day, the implementation of this still depends on the implementing agencies and local government units. The basis of all the guidelines existing now for rabies elimination in the country.
AO 2008-01 IRR of Anti-rabies act of 2007 National law that is collaborative across several government agencies and non-government organizations : Department of Health (DOH), Department of Agriculture (DA), Department of Education (DepEd), Local Government Units, and academe. This is a more cohesive documents on the guidelines in implementing RA 9482. Despite the law, at the end of the day, the implementation of this still depends on the implementing agencies and local government units. All the law has a corresponding IRR.Currently, the system such as National Rabies Information System (NaRIS) was created to complement the law, RA 9482, but its implementation has not achieved such purpose.
AO 2011-0002 Guidelines for managing rabies exposures secondary to bites by vaccinated dogs and cats This guideline is focused on the necessary steps to consider when exposed to rabies from vaccinated dogs and cats. This is useful to guide individuals in evaluating rabid animals despite being vaccinated. Despite the guidelines, at the end of the day, the implementation of this still depends on the implementing agencies and local government units.

There are Technical Working Groups (TWG) created by the DOH that is responsible for updating the documents necessary for rabies programs in the country. In the Philippines, all the clinical guidelines are based on World Health Organization (WHO). There are a lot of gray areas in rabies exposure hence, there are various guidelines for dfferent situations.

Eventhough there are numerous ABTCs in the country, there is only one regimen guideline and ABTCs are required to submit report to NaRIS to be able to aqcuire vaccines from DOH.
AO 2009-0027 Amendments of Revised guidelines on management of animal bite patients The amendments made to the law considered the recent studies and development from international organizations such as WHO and CDC. Ammendments should be made regularly given the developments in research. But this is not easy in the Philippines, hence this ammendments were made back on 2009. See: AO 2011-0002
AO 2007-0029 Guidelines on managing Bites cases by previously vaccinated dogs This is an elaborated guidelines for bite victims of animals previously vaccinate. These various guidelines shows that there is a case to case basis in terms of exposure to rabies. Despite the guidelines, at the end of the day, the implementation of this still depends on the implementing agencies and local government units. See: AO 2011-0002
AO 2014-0012 New Guidelines on the Management of Rabies Exposures This is a sample of updated guidelines on managing rabies exposure back in 2014, after the law has been signed on 2009. Ammendments should be made regularly given the developments in research. But this is not easy in the Philippines, hence this ammendments were made back on 2009. See: AO 2011-0002
PhilHealth for Animal Bite Package (Rabies Post-exposure Prophylaxis) This gives an oppoortunity for bite victims to access health services through PhilHealth. This can alleviate the burden of out-of-pocket availment of medical services especially that PEP is expensive compared to other kinds of vaccines in the market. This package shall NOT cover the following: a. Pre-exposure Prophylaxis b. Inpatient cases of animal bites and rabie s, which shall be reimbursed through inpatient benefit. c. Category I rab ies exposure and category II exposure NOT involving the head and neck d . Bites by rodents, guinea pigs and rabbits e. Jvianagement of adverse reactions f. Other conditions otherwise not mentioned in number 3. This was only released on 2014, just recently. There is an emphasize on the defraying the cost of post-exposure prohylaxis because the vaccine is expensive for an ordinary individual to shoulder - out-of-pocket.
Communicable Disease Control Chapter I - Management of Specific Diseases The context of this research is not in the Philippines but the general guidelines can be adapted to the situation in our country. The context of this article is not Philippines but there are general points that can be acquired for the intent of SPEEDIER. From time to time, the CDC is also conducting a study on rabies elimination and offer guidelines on the processes involve.
Driving progress towards rabies elimination: results of Gavi’s learning agenda on rabies and new WHO position on rabies immunization We can get international data on this article which can be an overview of rabies in the world. General data and no country specific information: Philippines. Through several years of lobbying to include rabies as one of the preventable diseases though vaccines for children below 5 years old, it was finally included in the international papers and Gavi’s also part of realizing the rabies elimination in various countries in the world.
Bureau of Animal Industry: Annual Report 2012 The report stated five provinces that were declared as Rabies free area based on the evaluation as per the implementing Rules and Regulations of Anti-Rabies Act (Republic Act 9482). This document did not mentioned the details as to how these 5 provinces became Rabies-free areas; interventions, programs of local government, context during the previous years, and the like. No comment.
Bureau of Animal Industry: Annual Report 2013 Compared to the annual report on 2012, this year, the agency were able to mentioned other areas as Rabies-free since 2008 up to 2013. There is also a little information on the interventions and programs of local government which lead them to become a Rabies-free area. No comment.
Bureau of Animal Industry: Annual Report 2015 The DA-BIA is constantly showing the list of rabies-free zone areas in the country in their annual report starting 2008. There is also a little information on the interventions and programs of local government which lead them to become a Rabies-free area. No comment.
Medium Term Plan 2012-2016 This is the plan of the government on how to eliminate canin rabies and human rabies in the country. It presents that mechanisms on how to achieve this goal through the help of various government agencies and non-government ogranization and stipulations of indicators for monitoring and evaluation. This is not in the document but given the present year, 2019, perhaps an annual report or overall report of this MTP should be accessible. No comment.
A Compilation: Good Practices and Lessons Learned for the National Rabies Prevention and Control Program Project Implementation in the Visayas This can be a good source of local resource in terms of rabies elimination in the country. It presents that good practices that can be adapted to other regions in Philippines. There is no explanation on how would there good practices can be applied to other area and be replicated or if necessary for the overall elimination of rabies in Philippines. This is one of the projects where Dr. Betsy was a part of.
Rabies vaccines and immunoglobulins: WHO position April 2018 Standard operating system (SOP) of delivering vaccines. Despite the lguidelines, at the end of the day, the implementation of this still depends on the implementing agencies and local government units. This is the document where the IBCM was introduced. Position papers released by WHO serve as guidelines at the same time.
Key facts on Rabies We can get international data on this article which can be an overview of rabies in the world. General data and no country specific information: Philippines. This documents also shows the key messages that can be utilized for rabies elimination programs.
The evaluation of operating Animal Bite Treatment Centers in the Philippines from a health provider perspective The authors recognized the importance of extensive rabies awareness initiatives, including the integration of rabies information into the school curriculum may reduce bite incidences, encourage dog vaccination and improve treatment seeking behaviour to reduce the risk of rabies in communities and allow more judicious use of PEP. Futhermore, the authors also mentioned that dividing resources optimally between these different strands will not only improve the cost effectiveness of the whole rabies prevention program, but is also the only way to reach the elimination of rabies from dogs and therefore an end to the threat of rabies to human health and the considerable financial burden that it incurs. The paper did not mentioned what are the various community awareness, dog bite prevention and responsible dog ownership and mass dog vaccination programs. No comment.
Evaluating Progress towards the Elimination of Canine Rabies: a management tool and its application in Latin America The developed tool provides guidance on how to progress efficiently towards control and elimination targets and tailor strategies to local epidemiological situations, while revealing insights into rabies dynamics and persistence. In Latin America, continued circulation in the poorest states puts neighbours at risk of re- emergence. Improved implementation and monitoring of mass dog vaccinations in these foci should minimize resurgence and catalyse progress towards elimination. The context of this article is not Philippines but there are general points that can be acquired for the intent of SPEEDIER. No comment.
PHE guidelines on managing rabies post-exposure cases (June 2018) We can get international data on this article which can be an overview of rabies in the world. General data and no country specific information: Philippines. UK is already rabies free but they still need to have a guidelines specially there were cases where rabies related deaths in their country is still being recorded due to migration of people from one country to another. And this is the purpose of PHE guidelines.
Surveillance to Verify Elimination of Dog- mediated Rabies The context of this research is not in the Philippines but the general guidelines can be adapted to the situation in our country in terms of strengthening surveillance The context of this article is not Philippines but there are general points that can be acquired for the intent of SPEEDIER. In Philippines, surveillance is really weak and NaRIS is only a system that records information on people who are consulting in the ABTCs and it does not cover those who are not consulting.
Infection with Rabies Virus International guidelines that each country can adopt Importation law and regulation of each country varies This document refered to as the bible of the veterinary because it has all the necessary information regarding the rabies virus and elimination.
